How many endangered species can you name?

How many of those are plant species?

Did you know that the number of plant species that have gone extinct in the past 250 years is more than twice the number of mammal, bird, and amphibian extinctions combined?

The Current Extinction Rate Has Reached Unprecedented Levels

Extinction rate is measured in Extinctions Per Species Per Million Years (E/MSY)

The current extinction rate (Extinction Rate After 1900) is roughly 500x the base historical extinction rate.

According to author Dr. Jochen Fründ of the University of Freiburg, “Chelostoma rapunculi is threatened directly, due to climate change, as well as indirectly, due to the disappearance of important food plants such as the harebell.”

Animals that closely depend on certain plant species during their entire life cycle appear to be particularly threatened. Therefore, insects will face a higher threat than many bird species, which tend to be more flexible in their food choice.

But there is hope.

Plant extinction is an extremely important and urgent issue. Luckily, there are people doing something about it.

Conservationists around the world are actively seeking out endangered plant species in the wild and porting them to controlled environments to ensure their survival.

One such duo is Adam Williams and Ben Nyberg, who are leading conservation and rediscovery efforts for endangered plant species in Hawaii, which is known as the endangered plant capital of the United States.

Recent research has also shown that similar conservation efforts around the world have had a positive impact. To date, there have been 431 plant species rediscovered in the wild that were once thought to be extinct.

Rediscovered vs Extinct Species

Plants are valuable resources of food, energy, medicine, and building materials. They are also essential to maintaining our planet's clean air. Relying on a handful of plant species makes us vulnerable to climate change and contributes to the endangerment of countless other species. We must make an effort to learn more about plant diversity and take action to protect it before it's too late.
